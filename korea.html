Nami Island

Nami Island, or Namiseom, is a picturesque destination located in Gangwon Province, South Korea. Known for its stunning natural beauty, the island is famous for its tree-lined pathways, particularly the iconic Metasequoia Lane, which is particularly enchanting in autumn when the leaves change color. Nami Island is a popular spot for both locals and tourists, offering a variety of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and art installations. Accessible by a scenic ferry ride, the island is also known for hosting the filming of the hit Korean drama " Winter Sonata," which has contributed to its romantic charm. With its lush landscapes and vibrant seasonal scenery, Nami Island is a perfect escape for nature lovers and anyone looking to enjoy a tranquil atmosphere.



































Gyeongbokgung Palace, SEOUL

The largest of the Five Grand Palaces in Seoul, was originally built in 1395 during the Joseon Dynasty. It showcases traditional Korean architecture and is surrounded by beautiful gardens and the picturesque backdrop of Mount Bugak. The palace features impressive structures like the Geunjeongjeon Hall, used for important ceremonies, and the National Folk Museum of Korea located within its grounds. Visitors can witness the Changing of the Guard ceremony and explore the serene courtyards, making Gyeongbokgung a vital cultural and historical site that offers a glimpse into Korea’s royal past.
