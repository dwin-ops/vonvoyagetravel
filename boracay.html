Puka Beach

a popular alternative to the party side of Boracay. Much different from the other parts of the island, the waves here can get a little choppy during the day. So, instead of the noise of party- goers, all you’ll hear are the natural sounds of nature. It’s a good idea to pack some snacks, lay out a beach mat, and enjoy a picnic while waiting for the famous Boracay sunset.































Ariel's Point

This Boracay gem is the perfect change of scenery from the Boracay you’re used to. Ariel’s point is most popular for their 15 meter high “diving boards” where adventure junkies can leap off to the clear waters below. But if you’re not up for the thrill, Ariel’s point is still worth the visit. Despite its increasing popularity, this area has one of the clearest waters and richest marine life in Boracay.

































Mount Luho

the highest point on Boracay Island in the Philippines, standing at about 195 meters (640 feet) above sea level. Known for its stunning panoramic views of the surrounding beaches and the vibrant blue waters of the Sulu Sea, it’s a popular destination for both tourists and locals. The trek to the summit is relatively easy and can be done via a well-marked trail or by a short drive. Once at the top, you can enjoy breathtaking vistas, especially during sunrise and sunset. There are also viewing decks and areas for relaxation, making it a perfect spot for photography and enjoying nature. The lush greenery surrounding the mountain adds to its charm, creating a serene escape from the busy beach scene below.
